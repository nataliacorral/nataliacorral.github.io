About me

UX/UI Designer | Industrial Designer

I’m a multidisciplinary designer focused on User Experience Design, Visual Design and Interactions.
I’m a selft-taught person who’s constantly learning and always looking for new challenges.

I became obsessed with every part of the product design process. From understanding users' needs, usability and interactions to the visual and emotional design.

My main objective is to create the best possible experience for the user.

WHAT I'M UP TO NOW

I am a constant seeker and a tireless professional, always in the search for new challenges that allows me to come up with creative and innovative solutions.

I'm currently taking part of the Globant Design Center 2020 LATAM Edition, a training program focused on UX/UI design. In there, I'm developing a digital product along with a team of designers and with the guidance of mentors.

Not long ago I became aware that accessibility is an issue that I was overlooking. As designers working in technology, It’s our responsibility to design inclusive products that work better for everyone.

My goal is to keep learning and be inspired to do more. Hopefully one day I will be able to create products that are truly inclusive.

I think it’s important for designers to understand how the products we create are built. That's why I decided to dive into the web development world, and I started to learn HTML5 and CSS3. This website was born to put what I learned into practice, which was actually a lot of fun!

I would also like to get to know a little bit more about how apps are built. I think that will allow me to design more efficiently, it will also help me to communicate better with developers.

In short, I'm always discovering new things.

NATALIA'S B SIDE: CERAMIST

That’s the moment when I let go of everything I know about design. I forget about pixel-perfect and let my hands flow.

My way of making ceramics is a reflection of who I am. I enjoy the process and really like to explore, even though sometimes I have to go back and start over.
I'm not just thinking about the final product but focusing on each step of the process.
